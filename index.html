The Problem

Knowledge that walks out the door

Every business runs on knowledge inside people's heads — not in systems, not in manuals. And that knowledge moves every time a person does.

↗

People move. Knowledge disappears.

Resignations, promotions, outsourcing, relocation. Every transition takes months of accumulated know-how — the workarounds, shortcuts, and unwritten rules that made things work.

◎

Owners can't see inside their operation.

They know outcomes — not processes. What does Ana in Finance actually do every Tuesday? Nobody knows, because nobody ever had to write it down.

⟲

AI workflows leave with employees too.

Employees build personal AI routines — custom agents, prompt libraries, automated workflows. When they leave, those go with them. A new version of the old problem.

How the Trust Gradient resolves the tension: The agent starts in Shadow Mode — proposing without acting. The employee approves or rejects every suggestion, giving them control. Shadow Mode is not a monitoring layer. It's a learning layer, and the employee is the one teaching it.

The Solution

Three pillars that make
ORGANA different

ORGANA is not a chatbot and not a wiki. It's an operating layer for your entire organization — built on three interconnected pillars.

Pillar 01

Agents that organize themselves

ORGANA doesn't just deploy agents — it builds an organization. Agents communicate with each other, run structured meetings, track their own reputation, audit failures, and retrain when they underperform.

Unlike any other tool, ORGANA agents coordinate as a team — they delegate tasks, escalate blockers, detect bottlenecks, and improve continuously without human intervention.
Pillar 02

Record once.
The agent handles it.

No manuals, no forms. Employees record task sessions — start, work, stop. The agent learns the real workflow: sequence, decisions, exceptions. This tacit knowledge is exactly what gets lost when people leave.

▸ Session: Weekly sales report
Excel → filter → sum → PDF → CEO

No extra effort — record and keep working
Knowledge owned by the company, not the employee
Pillar 03

Adoption by design, not by mandate.

The biggest risk for any AI product is employee resistance. ORGANA turns adoption into a game — with visible scores, benchmarks, and social recognition built into the product.
